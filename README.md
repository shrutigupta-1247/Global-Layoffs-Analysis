# Global Layoffs Data Analysi## Overview

This project presents an exploratory data analysis of global layoffs across companies, industries, and countries. The objective is to understand patterns in workforce reductions, identify the most affected sectors, and analyze how layoffs evolved over time.
The analysis is performed using Python and focuses on deriving meaningful insights through data cleaning, aggregation, and visualization.

---

## Objectives

The key objectives of this analysis are:

- To analyze global layoff trends over time
- To identify countries with the highest number of layoffs
- To determine industries most affected by workforce reductions
- To study company-level layoff patterns
- To examine the relationship between funding raised and layoffs
- To identify companies that experienced complete workforce reductions

---

## Dataset

The dataset includes information related to global layoffs such as:

- Company name
- Industry
- Country
- Date of layoff
- Total employees laid off
- Percentage of workforce laid off
- Funding raised
- Company stage
- Source
- Stage

- Python

---

## Data Analysis Process

The analysis follows these steps:

1. Data cleaning and handling missing values
2. Aggregation by company, industry, and country
3. Time-based analysis of layoffs
4. Calculation of total and average layoffs
5. Visualization of key trends and patterns

---

## Key Insights

### Time-Based Trends
- Layoffs peaked in January 2023 with the highest monthly total recorded.
- Layoffs occurred in distinct waves rather than a steady pattern over time.

### Country Analysis
- The United States recorded the highest total layoffs.
- India, Germany, the United Kingdom, and the Netherlands were also significantly affected.

### Industry Analysis
- The finance industry had the highest number of layoff events.

### Company Analysis
- Amazon recorded the highest total number of layoffs.
- Several companies underwent multiple rounds of workforce reductions.

### Workforce Reduction Severity
- Some companies recorded 100% layoffs, indicating complete workforce shutdowns.
- Layoff frequency and severity varied significantly across organizations.

### Funding vs Layoffs
- A weak positive relationship exists between funding raised and total layoffs.
- High funding did not necessarily prevent large-scale workforce reductions.

## Conclusion

This analysis highlights that global layoffs were heavily concentrated in the technology sector and peaked during early 2023. While large companies contributed significantly to total layoffs, the severity and frequency of layoffs varied widely across industries and organizations. The findings also indicate that funding levels alone were not a reliable indicator of workforce stability.
